About Me

For the past decade I have studied entomology, focusing on invasive insect and plant species interactions as a biolocial control field technician. My work has taken me to remote places throughout Oregon and Washington to collect, distribute and monitor populations of invasive plants that are being managed with host-specific insects.

Accomplishments

  • Created a visual screening aid for common insects for USDA field technicians in the NW Oregon region.
  • Field technician for the Oregon Department of Agriculture's study to determine the presense and abundance of brown marmorated stink beetle biological control agent Trissolcus japonicus.
  • Articulated black bear (Ursus americanus), california sea lion (Zalophus californianus), grey wolf (Canus lupus) and northern fur seal (Callorhinus ursinus) skeletons for curation in museum collections.
  • Assisted in the Oregon Dept. of Agriculture's internal study on the Linden aphid presense and abundance in NW Oregon (Eucallipterus tiliae)
